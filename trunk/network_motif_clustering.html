Network Motif Clustering Toolbox

The Network Notif Clustering Toolbox is a toolbox for clustering topological network motifs in large-scale, integrated networks. The clustering algorithm is based on rigorous mathematical results and has been validated on an integrated yeast interaction network. See the following publication for more information:

  • T. Michoel, A. Joshi, B. Nachtergaele and Y. Van de Peer, Enrichment and aggregation of topological motifs are independent organizational principles of integrated interaction networks (2011).

Version 2.0 contains functions for

  • Network motif clustering
  • Calculating network motif aggregation and enrichment significance
  • Generating random networks with fixed degree distribution and fixed motif count

A complete list of functions is available from the Contents file. Before starting, have a quick look at the Network, tensor and motif representations we use.
